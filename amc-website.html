The Three Pillars

A pipeline of well-prepared,
market-literate students.

01 · Professional Development

The fundamentals of a polished candidate.

LinkedIn, résumés, cover letters, behavioral interview skills, and foundational market knowledge. Several weeks dedicated to the table stakes of a high-finance career — built deliberately, not assumed.

02 · Technical Training

Hands-on exposure to every discipline.

Rotations through six tracks introduce members to the core functions, analytical tools, and day-to-day responsibilities of each. Real exercises, not abstractions — so you graduate AMC market-literate across the board.

03 · Mentorship & Community

Mentors, alumni, and the people ahead of you.

Individualized feedback within your cohort. Guest speakers from the Kelley Investment Banking, Investment Management, and Wealth Management Workshops. A network designed to follow you from underclassman to first job and beyond.

The Program · How It Works

A structured,
semester-long experience.

Phase 01

Professional Development

Several weeks dedicated to LinkedIn, résumés, cover letters, behavioral interview skills, and foundational market knowledge. The base layer every member builds on.

~ Weeks 1–4
Phase 02

Track Rotations

Members rotate through all six divisions — IBD, Research, PWM, S&T, CRE, and WM — getting hands-on exposure to the analytical tools and day-to-day work of each.

~ Weeks 5–10
Phase 03

Selective Interviews

A formal interview process — modeled on the recruiting cycle our members will actually face. Members earn placement into the cohort that best fits their interests and strengths.

~ Weeks 11–12
Phase 04

Cohort Placement

Ongoing mentorship, technical training, and recruiting support throughout the workshop and internship process — the launchpad into a high-finance career.

A Message From the Founders

Built over many dinners.

Bloomington, IN · Est. 2023

Our story began with a group of friends who, after leaning on each other through the recruitment process, realized how much Kelley was missing a true teaching-first and mentorship-based community.

Instead of expecting freshmen to choose a career path before understanding the industry, we envisioned a place where students could explore, learn, and grow with real guidance.

The idea — debated over many dinners and cemented at Lenny's on Kirkwood — became a commitment to give future students the supportive environment we wish we had.

Today, AMC is a dynamic undergraduate organization dedicated to providing hands-on financial education and professional development. We help underclassmen build a foundation in finance through an extensive bootcamp covering asset classes, market analysis, and investment strategies. Through stock pitches, financial modeling, and live debates, our members gain the practical skills needed for competitive fields like investment banking, research, sales & trading, and private wealth management.

At our core, we believe in the power of mentorship, collaboration, and success through hard work. Our founders and partners are committed to guiding members every step of the way — offering consistent feedback, expert-led workshops, and opportunities to network with industry professionals.
